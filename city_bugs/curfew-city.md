Curfew lasts from 11pm to 5am.

If you go out right after it lifts, most of the city is still asleep.
Trafic is abscent. You get to experience urbanist dream albeit ephemeral.

Here's the cycling lane on one of the most crowded bridges during regular time:

![](/assets/bridge-during-curfew.png)

You get the same for the roads.

For certain period of time, several bridges across the Dnipro river
were also closed for the cars.
Only pedestrians and cyclists were allowed.
As a result, riding on them was extremely satisfying as you could use all
the lanes and it was very quiet.

I semi-jokingly said that it would be good to leave those bridges closed
for the cars even after the war ends.
Especially, since some of them are in a questionable conditions.
However, most of my friends already bought into the car-centric city drama
and have their own cars, so naturally they didn't support this idea.

Cycling in Kyiv is still more of a leasure than a means of transportation.

Several light-weight bridges designed specifically for pedestrians
and cyclists would give you decent means for crossing the river and
provide induced demand.
Also they must be way cheaper to build than regular bridges, since
they don't need to handle as much weight.
